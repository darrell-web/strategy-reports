Why this plan will hit its KPIs

This plan does not rest on hope. It rests on three facts already in the data.

Fact 01 · Joyleaf has hit "Dispensary" #1 before

In January 2026, Joyleaf reached #1 for "dispensary" on the 2-mile benchmark — the first time after 25 months of effort. It held #1 in February. The April #4 reading is a regression, not a ceiling. The structural fixes in Pillar 1 reverse the specific signal drift that caused the slide.

Joyleaf has 2,224 reviews. The three competitors outranking on "dispensary" combined have 1,619 reviews (Plantabis 1,043 · Breakwater 234 · Dispensary of Union 342). Joyleaf is the most-reviewed dispensary in the 2-mile grid by a wide margin. The gap is not authority; the gap is keyword tokenization in the GBP name and on-page H1. That is fixable in code, in week one.

Q1 2026 conversion averaged 106.6% vs. 63.9% in Q1 2025 (+66.8% YoY). Direction requests up +28.5% YoY. Phone calls up +68.4% YoY. Every visibility point we add converts at the highest rate Joyleaf has ever recorded. May's lift compounds against an already-improving funnel.

4a. Q&A Seeding (Week 1) — eight owner-asked questions

Why it works: Q&A is one of the highest-leverage GBP signals — Google indexes the answers as part of the listing's keyword surface. Joyleaf has zero owner-posted Q&A right now. Eight questions × multi-keyword answers = a meaningful relevance addition with a single Week 1 task.

Q: Is Joyleaf a recreational dispensary?
A: Joyleaf Dispensary is a state-licensed recreational dispensary in Roselle, NJ — open to anyone 21+.
Q: What time does the dispensary close?
A: Our dispensary is open Mon–Sat 9 AM–10 PM and Sun 9 AM–8 PM.
Q: Is this dispensary licensed in NJ?
A: Yes — Joyleaf Dispensary holds NJ recreational license RE000124.
Q: What dispensary brands do you carry?
A: Our dispensary stocks Camino, Doinks, Simply Herb, Ozone, Garden Greens and more.
Q: Is this a women-owned dispensary?
A: Yes — Joyleaf Dispensary is proudly women-owned.
Q: Where's the closest dispensary to Roselle Park?
A: Joyleaf Dispensary is just 0.7 mi south of Roselle Park on E 1st Ave.
Q: Where's the closest dispensary to Cranford?
A: Joyleaf Dispensary serves Cranford via in-store pickup or fast delivery.
Q: Does the dispensary offer delivery?
A: Yes — our dispensary delivers across Union County (Roselle, Cranford, Linden, Elizabeth, and more).

Risk Register — what could break the plan

Honest accounting of what could prevent us from hitting May targets, and what we'll do about each.

R1 · Market-wide discovery decline continues
High

What: Discovery searches down 51.6% from Dec '24 peak. The decline is market-wide (every Dispenza client shows it). Likely Google algorithm and search-behavior shifts. Cannot be controlled.

Mitigation: Discovery is one of 11 KPIs. Even if Discovery misses 6,000+, the other 7 high-confidence KPIs are not exposed to this risk. The plan does not depend on Discovery alone.

R2 · Pillar 1 deployment delayed past May 3
High

What: Every day of delay reduces compound time. The 4-week post-deploy window is the foundation of the dispensary KPI math.

Mitigation: Dispenza dev team queued and on standby. Single ask for client approval (Ask 01). If approval slips past May 3, we re-baseline KPIs at mid-month check-in.

R3 · Competitor counter-push (Plantabis / Breakwater / Dispensary of Union)
Medium

What: Any of the top 3 dispensary competitors could simultaneously run their own SEO push and absorb our relevance gains.

Mitigation: Pillar 5 includes weekly competitor review-velocity monitoring. If a counter-push is detected, we accelerate Pillar 1 deploy (already shipped) + push GBP post cadence to 4/week + add a second hyperlocal page (Linden) in Week 4.

R4 · POS data still unavailable
Medium

What: Current POS export shows #DIV/0! errors. Without clean POS, we cannot tie SEO outputs to revenue impact for the end-of-month report. This makes proving the +120 customers / $1M target harder.

Mitigation: Ask 03 (POS export by May 15). If still broken at mid-month, switch to GBP-action-as-revenue-proxy reporting and document the gap explicitly.

R5 · Seasonal conversion-rate reset
Medium

What: May '25 conversion rate was 51.7% (down from Apr '25 68.8%). May historically sees a seasonal dip. Even though the funnel is fundamentally healthier than 2025, a similar drop would still drag the maintain-110%+ target.

Mitigation: Conversion rate is an output metric — Pillar 4 (delivery attribute, GBP posts, Q&A) directly counters seasonal drag by widening the action surface.

R6 · Adjacent-town review mentions need staff buy-in
Medium

What: The 30+ town-mention KPI depends on Joytenders consistently asking "Where'd you drive in from?" If staff doesn't internalize the prompt, it doesn't happen.

Mitigation: Treat this as a stretch metric. The plan does not depend on it for the headline KPIs. We'll provide a one-page training card and a Week 2 standup item.
